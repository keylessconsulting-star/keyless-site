Security

Three Layers of Security. Zero Shortcuts.

Layer 1: The Vehicle

The lockbox mounts inside the vehicle's own security perimeter. The car's door lock is the first wall. You can't reach the box without getting past the car.

Layer 2: The Box

A shielded, Faraday-caged key compartment. The fob inside can't be heard by relay-attack equipment — the #1 way modern cars get stolen. The box goes silent so the car stays put.

Layer 3: The Cryptography

AES-128 challenge–response. Every unlock starts with a new hardware-generated challenge that dies after one use — replayed codes are worthless. And the keys to the boxes never leave our servers: there is nothing on the phone worth stealing.

The car protects the box. The box protects the key. Every open is on the record.
